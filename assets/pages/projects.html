  • //Delphi -- at the /hack hackerearth 2016 hackathon, four teammates and i created a fully functional iOS application that determines whether you're able to go stargazing given current weather conditions (e.g. clear or foggy skies) at night.

    delphi provides a list of constellations given the user's location and season (e.g. northern hemisphere, fall), and stargazing events nearby (e.g. meteor showers, eclipses, etc). while helping my teammates with the iOS application, i also single-handedly wrote the fully functional web application with the same features as the iOS application during the duration of the hackathon, which can be found here . The source code for the iOS app can be found here .

    note: the ios app scrapes current user location using apple location frameworks, but delphi will not accurately grab your location and weather on web if you are using a vpn or a proxy as it scrapes user ip data. -- swift, xcode, javascript, html, css, jquery, forecast.io api, ipify api, openweathermap api



  • //LilyPad -- at the spectra 2016 hackathon, i worked with four other teammates to create a web application that allowed users to upload videos on the site using the youtube api. videos uploaded on the site were also uploaded to their youtube account after signing in with google authentications. users were also able to view videos to find potential roommates through various filters, such as queer friendly, price range, location, etc.

    i designed the ui/ux, set up the database, and worked full stack with a five person team -- python, flask, javascript, jquery, postgresql, twitter bootstrap, html/css, youtube api, google authentitcation

  • //Dandelion -- at the linkedin intern hackday 2016, three other people and i made a web application for a therapeutic comfort box. a user can upload videos, memories, notes from friends, journals, and create a list of people they can call (with pre-loaded hotline numbers) for them to look over in times of distress. made with mental health patients and people with anxiety in mind.

    a feature that i solely developed is the vent cloud. users can vent on a 'vent cloud' and their message will appear publicly for up to a defaulted 60 seconds (or a time determined by the user) before disappearing. multiple vent clouds from different users are able to appear on the page.

    i worked primarily on the vent cloud, the ui/ux, creating images and designing the page -- javascript, jquery, html/css, facebook authentication

  • //asterisk* -- i solo-ed at the hackdavis 2015 hackathon and created a google chrome extension that detects gender biased and derogatory language on the page or text file, highlights it, and offers more gender neutral and inclusive language when you hover over.

    for example, 'fireman' will be highlighted and 'firefighter' will be suggested, 'niece' will be highlighted and 'nibling' will be offered. users can also input their own list of words for the extension to detect. this is to help people be more aware of their everyday language that may be harmful towards the queer and trans community and any other marginalized communities -- javascript, jquery, html/css
